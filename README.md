## 📖 Introduction
This project focuses on developing digital literacy skills through practical activities. As a Student Digital Ambassador, my goal is to help peers understand the importance of using digital tools safely and effectively.  
Through this project, I explored creating infographics, building a digital portfolio, using coding and collaboration platforms, practicing professional email communication, and raising cybercrime awareness.  

---

## 📝 Tasks Overview

### Task 1: Digital Literacy Infographic
- Designed an infographic using Canva.  
- Covered topics: digital literacy, safe internet practices, professional online presence, and useful tools.  
- Challenge: balancing content and design without overcrowding.  

### Task 2: Digital Portfolio
- Created and updated profiles on **GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle**.  
- GitHub: Showcased projects and coding work with a profile README.  
- LinkedIn: Updated education and career interests.  
- Kaggle: Highlighted interest in data science and analytics.  
- Plan: Regularly upload projects, explore datasets, and connect with professionals.  

### Task 3: Coding & Collaboration Platforms
- Practiced coding challenges on **HackerRank**.  
- Designed a **Digital Literacy Quiz** using Google Forms linked to Google Sheets:- https://docs.google.com/forms/d/1XRLamhPqPIWDeVhyqAxOgD51vKwzoIoR5Fsha4aZISw/edit#responses.  
- Learned how digital tools encourage interactive learning and collaboration.  

### Task 4: Email Etiquette & Communication
- Wrote two professional emails:  
  1. Requesting an assignment extension.  
  2. Expressing interest in an internship.  
- Created a **Social Media Do’s and Don’ts checklist**.  
- Learned the importance of clear subject lines, respectful tone, and professionalism.  

### Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness
- Researched **UPI/Online Payment Fraud**.  
- Created a **case study** explaining how fraud occurs, who is targeted, and its consequences.  
- Developed a **Stay Safe Online prevention checklist**.  
- Key learning: scams rely on manipulation, not hacking.  
- Adopted habits like verifying links, avoiding unknown QR codes, and remembering that entering a UPI PIN always means sending money.
