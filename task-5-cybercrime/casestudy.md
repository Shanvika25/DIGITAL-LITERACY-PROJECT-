## Cybercrime Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud
UPI / Online Payment Fraud is a common type of cybercrime in which fraudsters trick people into sharing sensitive banking details such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or account information in order to steal money.
This type of fraud usually happens through fake phone calls, SMS messages, WhatsApp texts, or payment links that appear to come from trusted sources like banks, shopping apps, or customer support teams.
For example, a college student named Rahul receives a message saying that his bank account will be blocked unless he updates his KYC details immediately. The message includes a link that opens a fake banking website designed to look real. 
Rahul enters his mobile number, bank account details, and OTP. Soon after, he receives another call from a person pretending to be a bank executive who asks for his UPI PIN to “verify the account.” Once he shares the information, the fraudster quickly transfers money from his account.
People who frequently use digital payment apps such as Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm are commonly targeted, especially students and elderly people who may not be fully aware of online scams.
The consequences include financial loss, stress, loss of trust in online banking, and possible misuse of personal information for further fraud.
